Engagements

I work best where technology, AI, and execution need to become clearer.

If you are building a product, scaling an engineering organization, introducing AI into real workflows, or trying to improve execution quality, I can help through advisory work, focused strategy engagements, workshops, and speaking.

1:1 Executive advisory and operating reviews
Team Workshops for AI, delivery systems, and engineering execution
Stage Speaking that also drives strategic conversations

Advisory Engagements

Executive advisory retainer

Ongoing support for founders, CTOs, and leadership teams working through AI bets, product and platform choices, architecture direction, and execution-system design.

AI strategy sprint

A focused engagement to decide where AI belongs, what workflow it should improve, how success should be measured, and what an implementation path should look like.

Execution-system review

A review of delivery bottlenecks, trust gaps, team topology, planning quality, architecture-to-org alignment, and where execution is actually breaking down.

Architecture and platform review

A practical review of product and platform choices under cost, team, security, and implementation constraints.

Typical format: retained advisory, short strategy sprint, or a defined review over a few working sessions. I prefer selective, high-trust engagements over broad consulting arrangements.

Workshops and Team Sessions

AI beyond demos

For teams that need a practical way to evaluate, productize, and operationalize AI.

Execution systems

For organizations that want better delivery trust, clearer rhythm, and less friction between strategy and implementation.

Leadership communication

For technical leaders who need to frame decisions, constraints, and priorities with more precision.

These usually work best as half-day or full-day sessions with leadership, engineering, product, or mixed delivery teams.

Best Fit

Founders and executive teams

When product ambition, engineering reality, and AI pressure need to be turned into a coherent plan.

Engineering and product organizations

When delivery quality, architecture direction, and operating discipline need work at the same time.

Events and universities

When the audience wants substance on technology leadership, AI in practice, and systems thinking, not trend theater.
