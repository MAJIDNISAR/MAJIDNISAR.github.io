Speed is one of the most celebrated metrics in modern organizations, but in complex systems it can become deeply deceptive.

This issue argues that velocity on its own says very little about whether the system is moving in the right direction. When speed increases without alignment, teams often become more efficient at compounding the wrong decisions.

## What It Argues

- Reduced cycle time and higher throughput can hide directional problems.
- In complex systems, mistakes do not stay local. They cascade across layers and amplify through dependencies.
- Without coherence, speed turns into acceleration of error rather than progress.

## Why It Matters

Execution quality is not just about pace. It is about whether momentum is coupled to clarity, alignment, and sound decisions that can survive scale.
